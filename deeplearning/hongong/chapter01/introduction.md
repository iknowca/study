# AI, ML, DL

## What is AI?
Ai is a technology that creates computer systems with intelligence that can learn and infer like humans.
In 1943, Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts first introduced the concept of neurons, and in 1950, Alan Turing published the Turing test. In 1956, many scientists gathered at the Dartmouth AI Conference, and the outlook for AI was at its peak.
This period is called the dawn of AI.

In 1957, Frank Rosenblatt introduced the perceptron, and AI gradually developed. This period is called the golden age of AI.

However, due to the limitations of computer hardware performance, AI was limited to solving simple problems, and the first AI winter came. Next, the expert system appeared, and the AI boom returned, but soon faced its limitations, and the second AI winter came.

In the 2010s, AI reappeared with the advent of deep learning. Deep learning is a technology that uses neural networks to learn and infer patterns from data. Deep learning has shown remarkable performance in various fields, such as image and speech recognition, and has become the driving force behind the fourth industrial revolution.

## What is ML?


ML is a field of study that researches algorithms that automatically learn rules from data without having to program rules one by one. ML is divided into supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and reinforcement learning.


ML is closely related to statistics, and many ML algorithms are derived from statistics.


However, in the latest ML, the computer science field is leading based on experience rather than statistical or mathematical theory.


The representative ML library is scikit-learn, and the algorithms included in scikit-learn are stable and performance-verified, so they can be used relatively safely.

## What is DL?


DL refers to methods based on artificial neural networks among many ML algorithms.

This DL was born during the second AI winter, and in 1998, Yann LeCun succeeded in recognizing handwritten numbers by creating a neural network model. The model is called LeNet-5 and is the first convolutional neural network.


In 2012, Geoffrey Hinton won by overwhelming the existing ML methods in ImageNet. The model is AlexNet, and since then, convolutional neural networks have been widely used. (By the way, Geoffrey Hinton won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 2024 for his research on machine learning through artificial neural networks.)

In 2016, the match between Lee Sedol and AlphaGo marked the moment when computers beat humans in Go, which was considered impossible. Now, many DL models such as chatgpt and claude are emerging, and their speed is getting faster and faster.
